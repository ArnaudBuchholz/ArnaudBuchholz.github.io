The interview

I have read lots of articles about JavaScript interviews: what are the tricky questions, how you should answer them, what are the hot topics, which frameworks you should be looking at... Last year, I was in charge a building a team of JavaScript developers to work on an AngularJS application. I would like to share with you this experience in a fictionalized story based on real facts.
That day, the HR specialist came to me with a good news.
- Arnaud, we have a new candidate: he got a score of 180% on xxxx. Would you mind having a quick review of his test source that we can arrange an interview? - Sure, I will look at that. But please remember that a good score does not mean a good candidate. HR Specialist: No worries, just let me know if it's a go In this company, any potential candidate is first assessed using a remote test. Two questions must be resolved with a JavaScript program that the candidate must write. The questions were quite complex (it took me more time to understand the question than to answer it) and 2 hours were given to complete the answers. The interface is quite impressive as it records all the steps you go through: it also proposes testing tools so that you can input your own data and evaluate your program with it. Once achieved, the tool then executes a serie of tests to validate the answer and generates a report on the correctness of the answers as well as the efficiency of the code. At that time, my assumption was that when someone gets less than 160%, I would simply ignore him.
